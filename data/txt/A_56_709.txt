Programme budget implications of draft resolution A/56/L.45

Report of the Advisory Committee on Administrative and
Budgetary Questions

1. The Advisory Committee on Administrative and Budgetary Questions has
considered the statement submitted by the Secretary-General in accordance with rule
153 of the rules of procedure of the General Assembly on the programme budget
implications of draft resolution A/56/L.45, entitled “The situation in Central
America: procedures for the establishment of a firm and lasting peace and progress
in fashioning a region of peace, freedom, democracy and development”
(A/C.5/56/27). During its consideration of the statement, the Advisory Committee
met with representatives of the Secretary-General.

2. As indicated in paragraphs 4 to 14 of document A/C.5/56/27, the Secretary-
General requests the continuation in 2002 of provisions for a Political Affairs
Officer post (P-4) and one General Service post to continue to provide support to the
efforts of the Secretary-General in post-conflict peace-building in Central America.
The Political Affairs Officer would continue to monitor the Guatemala peace
process, providing support to the activities of the United Nations Verification
Mission in Guatemala (MINUGUA) and to the verification and good offices
functions carried out by United Nations Headquarters in respect of the peace-
building process in El Salvador. The Political Affairs Officer would also follow

to the salaries and common staff costs of these two posts amount to $188,500.

3. The Advisory Committee notes that an amount of $93,691,600 has been
included for special political missions under section 3, Political affairs, of the
proposed programme budget for the biennium 2002-2003 (A/56/6 (Sect. 3)),
pursuant to General Assembly resolution 55/233 of 23 December 2000.

4. Should the General Assembly adopt draft resolution A/56/L.45, the
Advisory Committee recommends that an amount of $188,500 be charged
against the provision for special political missions under section 3, Political
affairs, of the programme budget for the biennium 2002-2003.
